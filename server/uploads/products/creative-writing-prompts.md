# Creative Writing Prompt Pack
**15 PROMPTS** | Category: Creative

Unlock your imagination with prompts for fiction, poetry, screenwriting, and world-building.

---

1. **Story Starter Generator**
   "Write the first 300 words of a story in the {genre} genre based on this premise: '{one-line premise}'. Establish: a unique voice/narrative perspective, a sensory-rich setting (3 senses minimum), an inciting incident within the first paragraph, and a compelling character trait revealed through action (not description)."

2. **Character Bible Creator**
   "Create a detailed character profile for a {role in story}. Include: external traits (age, appearance, mannerisms, voice), internal landscape (core wound, secret desire, fatal flaw, hidden strength), backstory (formative event at age {X}, relationship that shaped them), arc trajectory (how they change from page 1 to final page), and 3 sample dialogue lines that reveal character."

3. **World-Building Questionnaire**
   "Answer these 15 world-building questions for a {genre} setting. Culture: values, taboos, rituals. Power: who rules, how they got power, who challenges them. Daily life: what people eat, wear, do for fun. History: the founding myth, a recent catastrophe, an unresolved conflict. Magic/Technology: rules, cost, who has access. Economy: currency, trade, poverty line."

4. **Dialogue Scene Writer**
   "Write a 500-word dialogue-only scene (no dialogue tags or description) between two characters: {character A} who {motive/goal} and {character B} who {motive/goal}. Use subtext — what they don't say matters more than what they do. Each line should reveal character, advance conflict, or expose a secret."

5. **Plot Twist Architect**
   "Design 3 plot twists for a {genre} story with this premise: '{premise}'. For each twist: setup (where the foreshadowing is planted), execution (the reveal moment), and aftermath (how it changes the story's direction). Rate each on a scale of 1-10 for surprise vs. inevitability (the best twists feel surprising yet inevitable)."

6. **Poetry Form Instructor**
   "Write a poem in {form} about {theme}. Form rules: {explain rules of sonnet/haiku/villanelle/free verse/etc.}. Include: one unexpected metaphor, a volta or turn, sensory imagery from 3 different senses, and a title that adds meaning to the poem rather than just labeling it."

7. **Scene Rewriter (Show vs Tell)**
   "Rewrite this 'telling' passage to 'show' the same information through action, dialogue, and sensory detail: '{telling passage}'. Rules: no emotion words (happy/sad/angry), no exposition dumps, reveal internal state through physical tells and environment interaction, maintain the same POV throughout."

8. **Genre-Bending Concept Prompt**
   "Combine {genre A} and {genre B} into a cohesive story concept. Create: a logline (one sentence), 3 key genre conventions you'll honor from each, 1 convention you'll deliberately subvert, the central conflict that emerges from the genre collision, and a tone/mood board description (3 visual references + 3 musical references)."

9. **Opening Hook Analyzer**
   "Analyze and improve this story opening: '{opening paragraph}'. Check: does it start in the right place (not too early), does it establish voice, is there a micro-tension in the first 3 sentences, is there a promise of what's to come. Provide 3 alternative opening lines, each using a different hook type: action, mystery, character, or philosophy."

10. **Writing Block Breaker**
    "I'm stuck on {project description} at the point where {describe block}. Give me 3 lateral thinking exercises to break through: (1) a 'what if' constraint change, (2) a prompt to write from an antagonist's POV, (3) a 10-minute sprint to write the worst possible version. Then give me 1 direct suggestion for moving forward."

11. **Fan Fiction / AU Creator**
    "Take the characters/setting from {existing work} and transplant them into {alternate universe/setting}. Define: what stays the same (core character essence, relationships), what changes (power dynamics, central conflict), and the new genre conventions. Write a 200-word scene showing this new dynamic in action."

12. **Short Story Outline Architect**
    "Outline a 3,000-word short story in {genre} using the 7-point story structure: Hook, Plot Turn 1, Pinch Point 1, Midpoint, Pinch Point 2, Plot Turn 2, Resolution. For each point: one sentence on what happens, the emotional beat, and the change in stakes. Premise: '{premise}'."

13. **Sensory Detail Engine**
    "Generate a sensory description bank for a {setting} scene. Sight: 5 specific visual details (color, light, movement). Sound: 3 ambient sounds + 1 sudden sound. Smell: 2 distinct scents. Touch: 3 textures. Taste: 1 taste (literal or metaphorical). Emotion: the dominant feeling this setting should evoke. Combine into one atmospheric paragraph."

14. **Narrative Voice Shifter**
    "Rewrite this passage in 3 different narrative voices: '{passage}'. (1) First-person unreliable narrator, (2) Third-person close (limited to one character's perspective), (3) Second-person immersive. For each: highlight 3 specific word/phrase changes that establish the voice, and explain how the reader's relationship to the story changes."

15. **Flash Fiction Constraint Generator**
    "Create a flash fiction challenge: write a {word count}-word story that includes: a {object} (must be central to plot), a line of dialogue that is also the title, a weather event that mirrors the internal conflict, and cannot use the word '{common word}'. Provide a 150-word example showing how these constraints can work together."
