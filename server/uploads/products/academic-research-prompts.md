# Academic & Research Prompt Pack
**15 PROMPTS** | Category: Academic

Accelerate your research, writing, and study with prompts for literature reviews, papers, and data analysis.

---

1. **Literature Review Synthesizer**
   "Synthesize the following {number} sources into a coherent literature review section on {topic}: {list sources with key findings}. Organize thematically (not source-by-source), highlight areas of consensus and disagreement, identify research gaps, and conclude with how your research addresses these gaps."

2. **Research Question Sharpener**
   "Evaluate and refine this research question: '{current question}'. Check: FINER criteria (Feasible, Interesting, Novel, Ethical, Relevant). Suggest 3 alternative formulations (descriptive, relational, causal). For each: identify the independent/dependent variables, unit of analysis, and methodological approach needed."

3. **Abstract Compressor**
   "Condense this paper/abstract into: (1) A 50-word version for a conference submission, (2) A 150-word version for a journal abstract, (3) A 3-line version for social media promotion. Preserve: gap statement, approach, key finding, and implication. Original: '{original text}'."

4. **Methodology Advisor**
   "Given the research question '{question}', recommend the most appropriate methodology. Consider: qualitative vs quantitative vs mixed methods, data collection technique (survey/interview/experiment/secondary data), sample size justification with power analysis, validity threats, and ethical considerations specific to {field}."

5. **Statistical Analysis Interpreter**
   "Interpret these statistical results for a {field} audience: '{output/results}'. Explain: what test was run and why, what the results mean in plain language, effect size and practical significance, limitations of the analysis, and 3 alternative interpretations a reviewer might raise. Assume the reader knows basic stats but needs application guidance."

6. **Peer Review Response Writer**
   "Draft a response to reviewer comments for a {journal} submission. For each comment: (1) Thank the reviewer, (2) Acknowledge the validity of the comment, (3) Explain what change was made (with line numbers), (4) If disagreeing, provide evidence politely. Maintain a respectful, collaborative tone throughout. Reviewer comments: '{comments}'."

7. **Grant Proposal Writer**
   "Write a 300-word grant proposal summary for {research project} targeting {funding body}. Sections: Problem/Research Gap (2 sentences), Objectives (3 bullet points), Methodology (3 sentences), Expected Outcomes (2 sentences), Broader Impact (2 sentences), Budget Overview (1 sentence). Align language with funding body's stated priorities: {priorities}."

8. **Data Visualization Advisor**
   "Suggest the best visualization type(s) for this dataset: '{data description}'. Variables: {list variables with types}. Research question: '{question}'. For each suggestion: chart type, what it reveals, potential misinterpretations to avoid, and accessibility considerations (colorblind-friendly, screen-reader compatible)."

9. **Thesis/Dissertation Outline Builder**
   "Create a detailed chapter outline for a {degree} thesis on {topic}. Chapter 1: Introduction (problem statement, research questions, significance, delimitations). Chapter 2: Literature Review (3-4 thematic sections + gap). Chapter 3: Methodology. Chapter 4: Findings (3-5 subsections by theme). Chapter 5: Discussion (interpretation, implications, limitations). Chapter 6: Conclusion. Include estimated word count for each."

10. **Citation & Reference Formatter**
    "Format these {number} references in {citation style} for a {field} paper: '{list of source details}'. Verify: each reference has all required fields (author, year, title, journal/publisher, DOI/URL), correct capitalization per style rules, and proper ordering. Flag any missing information needed for completeness."

11. **Conference Presentation Script**
    "Write a 15-minute conference presentation script for {paper title}. Timing: 0-2 min: Hook + research gap. 2-5 min: Methodology. 5-10 min: Key findings (3 slides max). 10-12 min: Implications. 12-15 min: Conclusion + future work. Include verbal cues for slide transitions and anticipated Q&A with prepared answers for 3 likely questions."

12. **Interdisciplinary Connector**
    "Connect {field A} and {field B} approaches to studying {topic}. Identify: shared concepts with different terminology, methodological approaches that could be cross-applied, conflicting assumptions that create research tension, and 3 research questions that genuinely require an interdisciplinary approach."

13. **Study Guide Creator**
    "Create a comprehensive study guide for {course/topic} exam covering: {list of topics}. For each topic: 3 key concepts (with one-sentence explanation), 1-2 example questions with model answers, common misconceptions, and memory/mnemonic aids. Format for active recall: questions on left, answers on right."

14. **Ethical Consideration Checker**
    "Identify potential ethical issues in this research design: '{description}'. Check: informed consent process, data privacy/anonymity, vulnerable populations, conflicts of interest, cultural sensitivity, dual-use concerns, and institutional review board (IRB) requirements for {country/institution}. Suggest mitigation strategies for each issue found."

15. **Knowledge Translation Summary**
    "Translate this research finding into actionable guidance for {non-academic audience}: '{finding}'. Format: What we found (2 sentences, no jargon), Why it matters (3 sentences with real-world implications), What to do differently (3 bullet-point recommendations), and Where to learn more (2 accessible resources). Max 300 words."
